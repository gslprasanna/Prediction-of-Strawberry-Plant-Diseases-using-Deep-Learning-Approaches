Description:

Powdery mildew is a common fungal disease that affects strawberry plants. The disease is caused by different species of fungi, such as Podosphaera aphanis and Sphaerotheca macularis, which grow on the surface of plant leaves, stems, and flowers, forming a white or grayish powdery coating.Powdery mildew can be favored by warm and humid weather conditions, as well as poor air circulation and high nitrogen fertilization.

Remedies for Disease

1.To control powdery mildew in strawberries, it is important to practice good cultural management practices, such as improving air circulation and reducing humidity by thinning out the canopy and avoiding overhead irrigation.
2.There are several fungicides available for the control of powdery mildew in strawberries, including sulfur, potassium bicarbonate, and horticultural oils.
